## 1. Introduction & Game Overview

Clash of Clans is a mobile strategy game built around four interconnected layers of play. A complete manual needs to address all of them, since a player's questions will eventually touch each one:

**Home Village** is the core game — building and upgrading a base around a Town Hall, training troops and heroes, and attacking other players' villages to earn resources, Trophies, and Stars. Everything else in the game ultimately supports or is supported by the Home Village.

**Builder Base** (unlocked at Town Hall 4 by repairing a Boat) is a separate second village with its own resources, its own troop roster, and its own asynchronous "Versus Battle" combat system. It runs on a different rhythm than the Home Village — faster, cheaper upgrades, no wait for an opponent to be online.

**Clan Capital** is a clan-wide cooperative mode: members pool a shared currency (Capital Gold) to build and upgrade a communal base spread across nine linked "districts," then use it to raid other clans' Capitals during weekly Raid Weekends.

**Clan** life ties everything together — Clan Wars, Clan War Leagues, Clan Games, donations, and the social/organizational layer that turns four separate mini-games into one shared hobby.

> **Academy Tip** — New players should not try to progress all four layers evenly. The Home Village is the spine of the game and should get priority; Builder Base, Clan Capital, and competitive Clan War League play are best treated as things to layer in once the Home Village routine (upgrade queue, army training, daily attacks) feels comfortable.

This manual follows the same structure the site already uses for other games: mechanical reference chapters first (buildings, troops, heroes), then applied strategy chapters (attacking, base building, wars), then the surrounding systems (clan management, events, economy) that give context to daily play.

A note on currency of information: Clash of Clans is an actively developed live-service game. As of this research pass (July 2026), the current maximum Town Hall level is **18** (released November 2025), the current hero roster is six heroes (the newest being **Dragon Duke**, added March 2026), and the trophy/league system underwent a major restructuring in October 2025 and again in April 2026 (see Chapter 12). Expect all of these numbers to keep moving — the manual should be revisited periodically rather than treated as a one-time snapshot.

## 2. Town Hall Progression & Economy

### The Town Hall as the game's clock

Every building, troop, and hero upgrade in the Home Village is ultimately gated by Town Hall (TH) level — a higher TH unlocks higher caps on existing content and, at many levels, brand-new buildings, troops, or defenses. The current maximum is **Town Hall 18**.

The general upgrade philosophy repeated across every stage of the game: **don't upgrade everything evenly.** Pick a small set of priorities (usually Laboratory, Heroes, and whatever unlocks the next attack strategy you're aiming for) and push those first, filling remaining builder time with defenses and economy buildings.

### Era-by-era overview

**TH1–5 (Foundations).** Core defenses (Cannon, Archer Tower), first Barracks/Army Camp, first storages. Dark Elixir unlocks in preparation for TH7. There isn't much strategic depth yet — the priority is simply unlocking the buildings and troops that later chapters assume exist.

**TH6–9 (Heroes come online).** Barbarian King unlocks at TH7, Archer Queen at TH9 — from this point, heroes should be upgrading continuously; they are widely considered the single best use of resources at every subsequent stage, since a hero that's mid-upgrade cannot be used in attacks. Suggested Laboratory order in this era: max the Dragon line, then P.E.K.K.A, then Elixir spells (Lightning before Healing). Dark Elixir Drill becomes a priority around TH7–9.

**TH10–13 (The long middle game).** Laboratory upgrades become the near-universal top priority, since Lab is the gate that everything else (troop levels for a chosen attack strategy) depends on. TH10 unlocks Siege Machine deployment via the Clan Castle. TH11 introduces the Grand Warden and Eagle Artillery. TH12 unlocks the Siege Workshop (build your own Siege Machines). TH13 unlocks the Royal Champion and the Scattershot defense.

**TH14–18 (Current endgame).** Multiple heroes, Hero Equipment (via the Blacksmith), and increasingly complex defenses (including "merged" defenses starting at TH16) mean upgrade planning needs to be more deliberate than earlier eras. Highlights: TH15 unlocks the Minion Prince hero, the Monolith defense, and is the unlock floor for the Dragon Duke (via Hero Hall level 9); TH16 introduces the **building-merging** mechanic (Ricochet Cannon, Multi-Archer Tower); TH17 adds the Multi-Gear Tower; TH18 (current max, since November 2025) adds **Guardians** (a switchable troop-like defense sitting atop the Town Hall, in Longshot or Smasher form) and the Super Wizard Tower. At TH18, a commonly suggested order is Laboratory → Hero Hall → Clan Castle → Spell Factory, keeping offense-relevant buildings ahead of pure defense to stay effective in wars and farming.

> **Priority** — At any Town Hall level, the Laboratory and active Heroes should almost never sit idle. Everything else (walls, defensive buildings, resource buildings) can be filled in around those two priorities.

### Resource Buildings

| Building | Role |
|---|---|
| Gold Mine | Passive Gold generation. |
| Elixir Collector | Passive Elixir generation. |
| Dark Elixir Drill | Passive Dark Elixir generation; has the fewest levels of any resource building and can be "Supercharged" for a temporary output boost. |
| Gold Storage / Elixir Storage / Dark Elixir Storage | Safely hold resources up to a cap; higher-level storages are a common raid target since they hold the most loot. |

The Town Hall itself also shelters a percentage of a village's loot, acting as a partial "safe" even when storages are exposed. Later in the game (TH14+), Gold and Elixir become less of a bottleneck than **Ore** (the currency for Hero Equipment, see Chapter 5) and Clan Capital resources — a useful thing to flag for players wondering why endgame guides talk less about Gold/Elixir than early-game ones do.

> **F2P Note** — Resource Potions (a Magic Item, see Chapter 13) temporarily boost Gold Mines, Elixir Collectors, and Dark Elixir Drills simultaneously; save them for periods when you can log in often enough to reset production, since the boost is time-based, not amount-based.

## 3. Defensive Buildings & Traps

### Core Defenses

These form the backbone of every base, present from early Town Halls and scaling in level cap as Town Hall rises:

| Defense | Function |
|---|---|
| Cannon | Basic ground-only, single-target. |
| Archer Tower | Ground + air, single-target, longer range than the Cannon. |
| Mortar | Long-range splash damage against ground only; cannot hit anything too close to it, and cannot hit air units at all. |
| Wizard Tower | Splash damage against ground and air at medium range. |
| Air Defense | High single-target damage, air-only. |
| Air Sweeper | Deals no damage; knocks back/redirects air troops away from the direction it's facing. |
| Hidden Tesla | Camouflaged until triggered; ground + air, single-target. |
| Bomb Tower | Ground-only splash damage; explodes for bonus area damage when destroyed. |
| X-Bow | Very long range, ground by default (can be fueled with Elixir to also hit air); can keep firing even while the player is offline if fueled. |
| Inferno Tower | Single-target defense whose damage ramps up the longer it stays locked onto one target — devastating against high-HP "tank" troops. |
| Eagle Artillery | Unlocked at TH12; extremely long range, high damage, but dormant until roughly 200 housing spaces' worth of troops have been deployed against the base. |
| Scattershot | Unlocked at TH13; splash damage against either ground or air per shot (not both at once), with damage falling off the further from the initial impact point. |
| Monolith | Unlocked at TH15; slow-firing single-target defense that adds bonus damage equal to a percentage of the target's max HP — built specifically to punish high-HP flying troops. |
| Spell Tower | Casts a supporting spell effect (Rage/Poison/Invisibility-style variants exist) over nearby defenses or troops. |
| Giga Tesla / Giga Inferno | The Town Hall's own built-in weapon, activating once the Town Hall itself is destroyed in battle. |
| Walls | Deal no damage; exist purely to obstruct and delay ground troop pathing. |

### Merged Defenses (Building Merging, TH16+)

Starting at TH16, certain defenses can be **merged** — combining two maxed lower-tier defenses into one stronger structure with new behavior:

- **Ricochet Cannon** (TH16) — merges two Cannons; shots ricochet from the first target to hit a second nearby enemy.
- **Multi-Archer Tower** (TH16) — merges two Archer Towers; can target up to three different enemies simultaneously.
- **Multi-Gear Tower** (TH17) — merges a Cannon and an Archer Tower; switches between a Long Range mode and a Fast Attack mode.
- **Super Wizard Tower** (TH18) — merges two Wizard Towers; longer range and chains lightning damage across many more targets than a standard Wizard Tower, at reduced damage per additional target.

### TH18 Signature Defense: Guardians

TH18's headline addition is the **Guardian**, a troop-like defense that stands on top of the Town Hall and jumps down to engage once an enemy enters range. Only one Guardian variant is active at a time, switchable via a Town Hall toggle:

- **Longshot** — ranged, hits ground and air, splash damage.
- **Smasher** — melee, hits ground and air, splash damage.

Guardians are leveled up by Builders like a normal defense, rather than being a Hero.

### Traps

| Trap | Function |
|---|---|
| Bomb | Hidden, ground-only, moderate area damage. |
| Spring Trap | Launches ground troops into the air and away, removing them from the fight. |
| Air Bomb | Same concept as the Bomb, but triggers on and targets air troops. |
| Giant Bomb | Larger footprint, bigger blast radius than the standard Bomb. |
| Seeking Air Mine | Very high single-target damage against one air unit. |
| Skeleton Trap | Spawns friendly Skeletons on trigger to distract or delay attackers. |
| Tornado Trap | Forces troops caught in its radius to move in a circle instead of advancing. |

Traps stay hidden until triggered, then automatically re-arm at no cost, and are always active during Clan Wars and Friendly Battles (there's no risk of a trap being "disarmed" because a Builder is busy).

> **Common Mistake** — New players often cluster all their traps around the Town Hall. Spreading traps across likely funnel points and compartment entrances (not just the core) punishes attackers earlier in their push, before they've committed their strongest troops.

| Baby Dragon | Flying splash attacker with bonus damage against air targets. |
| Miner | Burrows underground to bypass some defenses and Walls before surfacing; splash damage. |

| Troop | Role |
|---|---|
| Minion | Cheap flying swarm unit, fast and fragile. |
| Hog Rider | Fast ground troop that jumps over Walls and targets defenses directly. |
| Valkyrie | Melee troop that spins to hit multiple adjacent targets. |
| Golem | Massive-HP ground tank that splits into two weaker "Golemites" on death. |
| Witch | Ranged caster that continuously summons Skeletons to fight alongside her. |
| Lava Hound | Huge-HP flying tank that draws Air Defense fire; splits into "Lava Pups" on death. |

| Furnace | Stationary troop that continuously spawns small "Firemites" dealing damage-over-time to buildings. |

> **Academy Tip** — "Super Troops" (temporary, boosted variants like Super Barbarian, Super Wizard, Super Miner, etc.) are activated with Dark Elixir for a limited number of days and are a good way to try a stronger version of a familiar troop before committing Lab resources elsewhere.

### Elixir Spells (brewed at the Spell Factory)

| Spell | Function |
|---|---|
| Lightning Spell | Instant burst damage to a small area — good for sniping an exposed defense. |
| Healing Spell | Heals ground troops standing in its radius over time. |
| Rage Spell | Boosts troop damage and speed within its radius for a duration. |
| Jump Spell | Lets ground troops walk through Walls as if they weren't there, for a duration. |
| Freeze Spell | Freezes defenses or troops in place for a duration. |
| Clone Spell | Creates temporary duplicate copies of troops standing in its radius. |
| Invisibility Spell | Makes targeted troops untargetable by defenses for a few seconds. |
| Recall Spell | Pulls deployed troops back off the battlefield for later redeployment. |
| Revive Spell | Revives fallen troops on the battlefield. |
| Totem Spell | Drops a high-HP totem that distracts ground and air defenses for an extended period, without triggering traps or Clan Castle troops. |

### Dark Spells (brewed at the Dark Spell Factory)

| Spell | Function |
|---|---|
| Poison Spell | Damage-over-time cloud that also slows enemy troop movement and attack speed. |
| Earthquake Spell | Percentage-based damage to buildings only (stacks with multiple casts). |
| Haste Spell | Speeds up troop movement (not attack speed) within its radius. |
| Skeleton Spell | Summons a group of Skeletons at the target location. |
| Bat Spell | Summons a swarm of flying Bats at the target location. |
| Overgrowth Spell | Traps buildings within its radius so they become invisible and invulnerable for a duration. |
| Ice Block Spell | Immobilizes a single target — troop, Hero, or building — inside a block of ice. |
| Angry Spell | The newest Dark spell; enrages troops in its radius so they target Defenses specifically for a period. |

> **Academy Tip** — Spell timing matters as much as spell choice. Rage is most valuable dropped once troops are already clustered on a high-value target rather than the moment they're deployed; Poison is far more effective cast directly on enemy Clan Castle troops than on empty ground, since undefended, poisoned troops will actually walk away from the cloud if left with nothing to fight.

## 5. Heroes, Hero Equipment, Pets & Siege Machines

### Heroes (6 currently)

| Hero | Role |
|---|---|
| Barbarian King | Melee tank hero; ability summons Barbarian reinforcements. |
| Archer Queen | Ranged DPS hero; ability grants temporary invisibility with a damage burst. |
| Grand Warden | Support hero; ground mode grants an area buff aura, can switch to a flying mode that grants nearby troops temporary invulnerability. |
| Royal Champion | Ranged, highly mobile hero good at clearing defenses and traps from range; ability deploys a seeking shield. |
| Minion Prince | Dark-Elixir-flavored aerial/support hero; ability includes area splash damage and buffs to nearby Dark troops. Community guidance treats him as a strong "second-hero" or cleanup attacker rather than the ideal hero to open an attack with against a fresh base. |
| Dragon Duke | The newest hero (added March 2026), unlocked at Hero Hall level 9 (TH15). A second flying hero that becomes "enraged" — a major damage boost, plus reduced trap damage — specifically when no other friendly flying troops are nearby, making him a lone-wolf, assassin-style hero rather than one that wants to be embedded in a big air army. |

### Hero Equipment

Hero Equipment is built and upgraded at the **Blacksmith** using **Ore**, and each Hero can carry two equipped pieces at once, chosen from that Hero's unlocked pool.

Two rarities exist:

- **Common Equipment** — unlocked by upgrading the Blacksmith itself.
- **Epic Equipment** — unlocked through special events (typically tied to the seasonal Gold Pass track); generally stronger and more build-defining than Common pieces.

Ore comes in three tiers of increasing rarity — **Shiny Ore** (used at all levels), **Glowy Ore** (needed for key milestone levels of both rarities), and **Starry Ore** (rarest, needed for top Epic-gear milestones) — earned mainly through Star Bonuses and Clan Wars. Unlike building upgrades, Blacksmith upgrades complete instantly once enough Ore is available.

> **Advanced** — Community consensus as of mid-2026 is that at Town Hall 15 and above, Hero Equipment quality matters at least as much as raw troop levels. A maxed army can still fail an attack if the attacking Heroes are running weak or mismatched equipment, so Ore spending deserves the same deliberate planning as Laboratory upgrades.

## 6. Attack Fundamentals & Strategies by Town Hall

### Fundamentals every attacker needs

**Scouting.** Before deploying anything, check the enemy's Town Hall placement, how much defensive coverage overlaps, hints of trap density, and — critically — the visible Clan Castle troop indicator. Identify the side of the base with the weakest/thinnest defensive coverage before choosing where to commit.

**Funneling.** Troops path toward the nearest building and route around Walls rather than through them, so left alone they tend to spread out along a base's outer edge instead of driving into the core. A "funnel" is created by clearing or pulling outer buildings on two sides of an entry point so the main army is compressed into a single lane toward the center. Weak funneling is one of the most common reasons an otherwise-good army fails to reach the core.

**Troop roles.** Compositions generally combine three roles: **tanks** that absorb damage and shape pathing (Golem, Root Rider, Super Yeti, Electro Titan, P.E.K.K.A), **support/utility** troops that disable defenses or clear clustered buildings (Witches, Bowlers, Throwers, Healers), and **damage/finishers** that punch through remaining structures (Dragons, Miners, Hog Riders, Balloons). Heroes act as flexible hybrid pieces, tanking, sniping specific defenses with their equipment, or mopping up.

**Spell timing.** Rage is most valuable dropped where troops are already clustered on a high-value target, not the instant they're deployed. Freeze is best saved for the highest-DPS defenses (Inferno Towers, Monolith, X-Bows) or for Clan Castle troops that couldn't be lured out. Poison should land directly on Clan Castle troops — a poisoned defender left with nothing to fight will actually walk out of the cloud, so attacking troops need to engage while the poison is still active. Invisibility is often preferred over Freeze for shepherding slow or fragile troops (Electro Dragons, Balloons, Root Riders) through high-danger zones, since it removes targeting entirely rather than just disabling the defense for a while.

**Handling Clan Castle troops.** Standard practice is to **lure** defending Clan Castle troops out with a handful of cheap units, ideally to a spot outside the range of the base's other defenses (corners near Builder Huts are commonly cited as safe luring spots), before committing the main army.

**Star ratings & destruction percentage.** Up to three stars are available per attack: one for destroying the Town Hall, one for reaching roughly 50% destruction, and one for reaching 100% destruction — a true 3-star requires both the Town Hall destroyed and full (or near-full) destruction. In Clan Wars, a clan's overall percentage is the sum of each member's *best* single attack against their assigned target, not every attempt made.

> **Common Mistake** — Dropping the whole army in one spot without first shaping a lane. Clear or pull outer buildings on the chosen side before committing the main force, rather than spamming troops and hoping they converge on their own.

### Strategies by Town Hall tier

**TH1–6 (early game).** Mass Barbarians give way to Giant/Archer/Wall Breaker combinations as Wall Breakers, Balloons, and eventually Healers unlock — a "Giant-Heal" push (Giants tanking, kept alive with Heal Spells) is a common TH6 milestone.

**TH7–10 (mid game).** "Zap Dragons" (Dragons plus Lightning Spells to clear Air Defenses first) is a long-standing, highly consistent TH7 and TH10 strategy. TH8 commonly uses Golem/Valkyrie (Golems tank while Valkyries cut through the base behind them). TH9 leans on mass Witches, using multiple Golems as tanks while summoned Skeletons apply sustained pressure.

**TH11–14 (late game).** This is where strategy depth opens up: **Queen Charge Hybrid** (the Archer Queen, backed by equipment and Clan Castle support, charges ahead to snipe key defenses and open a lane, followed by a mixed ground-and-air cleanup force) becomes the recommended next step beyond simpler mass-troop comps. Other strategies that remain relevant through this range include LavaLoon, Electro Titan Smash, Super Bowler Smash, Electro Dragon spam, DragBat, and Sui Lalo (timing-dependent Lava Hound/Balloon variants).

**TH15–18 (current endgame).** Named strategies shift more often here as new heroes and troops (Dragon Duke, Root Rider, Thrower) enter the meta. As of this research pass, commonly cited options include Royal Champion Charge into Mass Dragons or Mass Super Yetis, Root Rider-based ground pushes (often paired with Throwers for ranged support), Fireball Super Yeti Smash, and Hydra-style hybrid air attacks using Battle Blimps. At TH18 specifically, Root Rider + Royal Champion Charge compositions are frequently cited as the most consistently reliable option across varied base designs, while Mass Thrower Smash and Rocket Electro Dragon + Dragon Duke comps are cited as strong alternatives for War and Clan War League play.

> **Advanced** — Named "best" strategies shift with nearly every balance patch, and different strategy sites often disagree on which is "#1" for a given Town Hall. Treat any specific named strategy as a starting template to learn the underlying troop-role logic from, not as a fixed recipe to copy indefinitely.

## 7. Base Building Philosophy

The same building blocks — Walls, compartmentalization, trap placement, splash-defense coverage — apply to every base type. What changes is **what the layout is trying to protect**, and how aggressively it's tuned against a known attack meta.

### Farming Base

Goal: protect **resources**, not Trophies. A common pattern is placing the Town Hall near the edge of the base (letting raiders take an easy partial win there) while burying storages deep in the core — deliberately sacrificing Trophies in exchange for loot security. Compartmentalization matters even more here than elsewhere, since a maze of small walled pockets separates troops and blunts splash damage from a single entry point.

### Trophy / Ranked Base

Goal: minimize the attacker's star count to protect league standing, rather than protect resources specifically. At the highest tiers this means pushing the Town Hall as far into the base as possible, ringing it with Inferno Towers or Scattershots, clustering Air Mines around key anti-air defenses, and deliberately engineering long, offset troop pathing to punish the most common attacks seen at that level (for example, multiple Inferno Towers guarded by Skeleton Traps specifically to counter Queen Charge and Hog Rider/Miner pushes). The explicit design philosophy accepts a 2-star loss as a "win" if it denies the third star.

### War Base

Goal: deny stars — especially the third star — by wasting the attacker's time and breaking clean troop pathing, more than protecting resources. Competitive War Base design favors an asymmetrical layout with large "dead zones" around key defenses (Town Hall, Eagle Artillery/Monolith, Spell Towers) specifically to break troop pathing, spreading the most valuable defenses far apart from each other so a single spell combo can't grab them all cheaply, and sometimes facing Air Sweepers away from the Town Hall to lure air troops into dense Seeking Air Mine clusters.

### Clan War League (CWL) Base

Functionally an intensified War Base, tuned specifically against whatever the current top-meta attacks are for that season (recent examples: Root Riders, Electro Titans, Clone-Blimp bursts, LavaLoon hybrids). The same dead-zone and defense-spreading principles apply, but curated and play-tested against the specific strategies likely to be used by similarly-matched opponents in the tournament format.

### Common base-building mistakes

- Building against a base edge or corner, which can simplify attacker pathing and leaves exposed "dead grass" zones.
- Placing buildings too close together, letting a single splash-damage hit strike multiple structures at once — leaving at least one tile of gap between buildings helps.
- Random, unplanned building placement rather than deliberate compartmentalization.
- Forgetting that hero attack ranges (particularly the Archer Queen's) can snipe defenses from outside a compartment that would otherwise seem protected.
- Leaving the Town Hall exposed on a Trophy, War, or CWL base — the opposite of the deliberate approach used on Farming Bases.

## 8. Clan Wars & Clan War Leagues

### Regular Clan Wars

A Clan War runs **47 hours total**: 23 hours of **Preparation Day** followed by 24 hours of **Battle Day**.

- During Preparation Day, war bases can be rearranged (using only pieces already owned in the regular village), Clan Castle troops can be donated for war use, and members can see the matched clan's roster — but not the arranged base layouts, which stay hidden until Battle Day.
- On Battle Day, bases become visible and attackable. Each participant gets **2 attacks** and cannot attack the same base twice; if two clanmates both attack the same target, only the better of the two results counts toward the clan's total.
- **War sizes** available: 5v5, 10v10, 15v15, 20v20, 25v25, 30v30, 40v40, and 50v50.
- **Matchmaking** sums a hidden "war weight" value across every base element (defenses, Walls, traps, troops, spells, Heroes) for all participants, then looks for an opposing clan with a similar total. A 2026 rework weighted offensive upgrades (including Hero levels) more heavily, which has significantly weakened older "engineered base" tricks (deliberately rushing defenses to lower weight while keeping troops/Heroes maxed).
- **Win condition:** most total Stars wins; if Stars are tied, higher total destruction percentage wins; if both are tied, it's a draw.
- **War bonus loot:** winning members receive a large bonus (roughly 7x the base's available loot) delivered to the clan's Treasury, provided the attacker got at least 1 Star; losing members still receive a bonus, but at half the value. This can be increased further by the clan's War Bonus Extra Loot perk (see Chapter 9).

### Clan War Leagues (CWL)

CWL is a **monthly, week-long league tournament**, separate from regular Clan Wars. Signup requires a 2-day window and between 15 and 50 enrolled members; once a clan signs up, it cannot run regular Clan Wars or Friendly Wars until that CWL concludes.

- Clans are grouped into pools of **8 clans** within a similar league tier, fighting **one war per day for 7 days**.
- Each player gets exactly **1 attack per war day** (not 2, as in regular wars).
- War size is 15v15 or 30v30.
- Standings within the group of 8 are ranked by total Stars earned across the week (individual Stars plus a bonus for each war the clan wins); the top clans in a group are promoted, the bottom clans demoted, with higher leagues typically promoting fewer clans per cycle to slow the climb into the most competitive tiers.
- **Rewards** are paid as League Medals, spendable in the in-game League Shop. Each player's medal total depends on the clan's final group placement, a personal "Star Yield" multiplier based on their own Stars earned that week (capped once a player reaches 8 Stars), and Bonus Medal Packages distributed by Leaders/Co-Leaders after the week ends.

> **Advanced** — Because personal medal yield caps at 8 Stars, well-run clans deliberately bench players once they hit that cap and rotate in fresh members, since every Star beyond 8 stops adding to that individual's medals but still helps the *clan's* total war-win count (and therefore everyone's bonus packages).

### CWL strategy notes

- Build a core lineup of the most reliable attackers for the toughest matchup days, keeping additional enrolled members as bench flexibility.
- Since each player gets only one attack per war all week, watching earlier clanmates attack similar bases (trap placement, defensive Clan Castle composition, pathing) before committing your own attack — often called scouting or "sniping" a base — is standard practice rather than mirror-attacking blind.
- Assign attackers to whichever enemy base best matches their army composition, not simply the base directly across from them on the map.
- Prioritize guaranteed 1–2 Stars over risky 3-star attempts once a clan's promotion or demotion position is on the line.

### Friendly Wars & Challenges

- **Friendly Wars** let a clan challenge another clan of its choosing (rather than being matched automatically); troops aren't consumed except for Clan Castle troops/spells used defensively, and there's no resource loot or Clan XP — purely for practice.
- **Friendly Challenges** are one-off practice attacks against a specific clanmate's base, useful for testing new layouts or attack strategies risk-free.
- A clan can only be doing one of regular Clan Wars, CWL, or a Friendly War at any given time.

## 9. Clan Management & Perks

### Clan Roles

| Role | Permissions |
|---|---|
| Leader | Full control — starts/declares wars, changes all clan settings, promotes/demotes/kicks anyone, transfers leadership. Automatically assigned to whoever founded the clan; can auto-transfer to a Co-Leader after 60+ days of inactivity. |
| Co-Leader | Nearly all Leader powers except a few top-level settings — can invite/kick members, promote/demote up to Elder, start wars, and manage war/CWL rosters. |
| Elder | Can invite new members and kick regular Members (not other Elders, Co-Leaders, or the Leader). Typically an earned trust rank for active, reliable players. |
| Member | Full participation in Wars, CWL, Clan Games, and donations, with no administrative permissions. |

> **Academy Tip** — Keep the Co-Leader count small and reserved for genuinely trusted, active veterans; over-promoting risks mismanaged wars or rosters. Use Elder as the reward tier for reliable regulars who don't need administrative power.

### Clan Perks (unlocked via Clan Level / Clan XP)

Clan XP accumulates from war wins, Clan Games tiers, and Raid Weekends, raising the clan's level and unlocking:

- **Donation Limit** — raises how many troops (and eventually spells/Siege Machines) can be donated per request above the base amount.
- **War Loot Bonus** — improves at Clan Levels 3, 5, 7, and 9, adding extra bonus war loot on top of the base war-win bonus.
- **Donation Upgrade** — automatically upgrades the level of troops/spells donated to a clanmate; improves at Clan Levels 5 and 10.

### Clan Games

A recurring monthly event offering a shared set of challenge tasks (attacking, donating, upgrading, spending resources, and similar) split across Home Village and Builder Base tasks. Completing tasks earns points that count toward the clan's shared total; six reward tiers unlock as the clan's combined point total rises, each offering a choice of one of three rewards (resources, Gems, or Magic Items). Individual players can earn up to a large personal point cap per event, with an extra bonus reward pick available after reaching a set personal threshold.

> **Academy Tip** — Save "bankable" tasks — troop donations, spell brewing, obstacle clearing — for the days just before Clan Games starts, so points can be burned through quickly once the event opens. A fuller, more active roster clears the shared tier thresholds faster and lets everyone play at a relaxed pace rather than grinding to their own personal cap alone.

## 10. Builder Base (Village 2)

### Overview

Builder Base is a fully separate village unlocked by repairing a **Boat** on the Home Village shore (requires Town Hall 4+). It runs on its own resources — Builder Gold and Builder Elixir — with its own storages, mines, and a unique **Gem Mine**. It's managed by a distinct **Master Builder** character rather than the Home Village's Builders.

The mode was substantially reworked in the **"Builder Base 2.0"** update (May 2023), which remains the current foundation of how it plays: nearly every troop gained an active or passive special ability, the old head-to-head battle system was replaced with an asynchronous system, and loot generation was decoupled from stealing (see Versus Battles below). A further change in **May 2026** added a **second, dedicated Builder** for Builder Base upgrades — available once all Home Village Builders are unlocked — letting two upgrades run simultaneously in the Builder Base for the first time.

### Builder Hall Progression

Current maximum: **Builder Hall 10 (BH10)**. Key unlocks along the way include the Battle Machine (BH5, the first Hero Machine), a second attackable base "stage" via O.T.T.O's Outpost (BH6), the Battle Copter (BH8, second Hero Machine), B.O.B Control (BH9, path to a permanent 6th Home Village builder), and the X-Bow defense plus general level-cap increases (BH10).

### Buildings

Resource buildings mirror the Home Village concept (Gold Mine, Elixir Collector, storages, plus the unique Gem Mine). Army buildings include the Builder Barracks (instant troop training, no wait), Army Camp, Star Laboratory, the two Hero Machine Altars, a Reinforcement Camp, and a Healing Hut. Defensive buildings include Cannon, Double Cannon, Archer Tower, Hidden Tesla, Firecrackers, the high-damage **Crusher** (Builder Base's single hardest-hitting defense, though outranged by ranged troops), the Guard Post (garrisons live defensive troops), Air Bombs, Multi Mortar, Giant Cannon, Mega Tesla, Lava Launcher, and the X-Bow.

### Troops

Twelve troops are currently trainable at the Builder Barracks, most carrying a signature active or passive ability: Raged Barbarian, Sneaky Archer, Boxer Giant, Beta Minion, Bomber, Baby Dragon, Cannon Cart, Night Witch, Drop Ship, Power P.E.K.K.A, Hog Glider, and Electrofire Wizard (the newest and most flexible, switching between a ramping single-target beam and a chain-lightning mode).

### Hero Machines

Builder Base's two "heroes" are called Hero Machines rather than Heroes and both use a charge-up ability system (holding the ability charges through 3 levels of strength before activating):

- **Battle Machine** (BH5) — smashes through defenses with an electrified hammer; its ability briefly stuns itself but restores health and grants a temporary damage/speed buff.
- **Battle Copter** (BH8) — a flying Hero Machine whose ability is a nosedive attack dealing a powerful area explosion on impact.

### Versus Battles

From Builder Hall 6 onward, an enemy base has **two stages**: Stage 1 is attacked first, and only once engaged does Stage 2 (anchored by O.T.T.O's Outpost) become visible and attackable — meaning army composition needs built-in versatility, since the second stage's defenses can't be scouted in advance. Each stage awards up to 3 Stars based on percentage destruction, for a maximum of 6 Stars per attack.

The biggest conceptual difference from the Home Village: **loot is never stolen**. Attacking earns Builder Gold from a generated pool; successfully defending earns Builder Elixir. A daily Star Bonus (requiring a League-dependent number of Stars) grants a large one-time bonus, after which players can keep attacking for further loot. There is no daily attack cap.

> **Academy Tip** — Because Stage 2 is always hidden until Stage 1 is cleared, avoid hyper-specialized "one-trick" armies in Builder Base; a mix of tank, splash, and single-target troops handles an unknown second stage far better than a narrow, single-purpose composition.

*(Sources: Supercell official blog — May Update patch notes, Attacking and Defending in Builder Base 2.0, New Battle Copter and Troop Abilities; Clash of Clans Fandom Wiki — Boat, Builder Hall, Builder Base Troops, Battle Machine, Battle Copter, Versus Battles, Builder Battles pages; Blueprint CoC and Base Drop Builder Base attack strategy guides.)*

## 11. Clan Capital & Raid Weekends

### Overview

Clan Capital is a clan-wide cooperative mode unlocked once a clan reaches **Clan Level 2** (individual players need **Town Hall 6+** to participate). The whole clan repairs and upgrades a shared base — **Capital Peak** plus eight surrounding **Districts** — using a currency called **Capital Gold**, earned mainly through Raid Weekends and passively through the **Forge** (a Home Village building that converts Home Village or Builder Base resources into Capital Gold over a fixed 3-day crafting time).

### Capital Hall & Districts

The **Capital Hall** is the central progression gate, currently maxing at **level 10**; reaching level 2 unlocks Raid Weekend participation and gives the Hall its own long-range splash defense. Each Capital Hall level from 2 through 9 unlocks one new District, in order: **Barbarian Camp, Wizard Valley, Balloon Lagoon, Builder's Workshop, Dragon Cliffs, Golem Quarry, Skeleton Park, and Goblin Mines** — the last two added in October 2023 and, as of this research, still the most recent expansion of the district roster. Each District has its own District Hall gating its internal building levels, plus fixed non-editable terrain (rivers, cliffs, bridges) that shapes deployment during attacks.

### Capital Troops & Spells

Troops and spells are trained per-district and shared clan-wide (an individual's Home Village or Builder Base progress doesn't matter here — the clan's own Barracks/Spell Factory levels do). Current roster highlights: Super Barbarian, Sneaky Archers, Super Giant, Battle Ram, and Minion Horde (Barbarian Camp); Super Wizard (Wizard Valley); Rocket Balloons, Skeleton Barrels, and Flying Fortress (Balloon Lagoon); the Raid Cart siege unit and Super/Power P.E.K.K.A (Builder's Workshop); Hog Raiders and Super Dragon (Dragon Cliffs); Mountain Golem (Golem Quarry); Inferno Dragon (Skeleton Park); and Super Miner plus Mega Sparky (Goblin Mines). Capital spells include Healing, Jump, Lightning, Frost (a Clan-Capital-exclusive slow effect, distinct from the Home Village Freeze), Rage, Graveyard, and Endless Haste.

### Raid Weekends

Raid Weekends run weekly, **Friday 7:00 AM UTC to Monday 7:00 AM UTC**. Raiding is one-directional — your clan attacks a matched opponent's Capital without a simultaneous counter-raid — and damage to a district persists between separate attacks on it. Each player gets a baseline of **5 attacks**, with the first successful clear of any district granting **1 bonus attack** (maximum 6 total). Capital Gold is earned per building destroyed and is newly generated, not stolen from the defending clan.

**Raid Medals** are earned from both offense (districts destroyed, scaled by attacks used and capped per attack) and defense (housing space destroyed by your own clan's district defenses, capped separately), pooled and distributed equally to every participating clan member once the weekend ends. Medals can be spent with the **Trader** or used to reinforce your Clan Castle with strong troops.

> **Academy Tip** — Use all 6 available attacks every weekend; medals scale directly with attacks used, so partial participation directly costs the whole clan medals. Coordinate in clan chat so a district's finishing blow isn't wasted on overkill once it's already close to destroyed — only the first clear grants the bonus attack.

*(Sources: Clash of Clans Fandom Wiki — Clan Capital, Capital Hall, Clan Capital Troops, Clan Capital Spells, Raid Weekends, Forge pages; Supercell official blog — Clan Capital Changes; Theria Games and GamingOnPhone Clan Capital troop guides.)*

## 12. Ranked Mode & League System

### Battles vs. Ranked Battles

Since an October 2025 restructuring, Home Village multiplayer is split into two separate modes:

- **Battles** — casual, unlimited attacks, matched by Town Hall level; this is effectively the "farming" mode, with simplified Shield rules and no effect on league standing.
- **Ranked Battles** — unlocked at **Town Hall 7**, matched by league/Trophy standing, with a limited number of attacks and defenses per week. Trophies are earned or lost only here.

Both modes count toward the daily Star Bonus, but only Ranked Battles affects league position.

### League Ladder

The current ladder runs through 33 named sub-league tiers before reaching Legend status: **Unranked → Skeleton (1–3) → Barbarian (4–6) → Archer (7–9) → Wizard (10–12) → Valkyrie (13–15) → Witch (16–18) → Golem (19–21) → P.E.K.K.A (22–24) → Titan/Electro Titan (25–27) → Dragon (28–30) → Electro/Electro Dragon (31–33) → Legend**. Each Town Hall level has a fixed minimum league it can't be demoted below (a "League Floor"); upgrading Town Hall mid-tournament can auto-promote a player to their new floor.

### Legend League (April 2026 restructuring)

Legend League is now split into three tiers, each with identical Star Bonus and League Bonus rewards but different **Battle Modifiers** (defense/attack buffs and penalties) and structure:

| Tier | Format | Attacks | Promotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legend III | Weekly Tournament | 24 battles/week | Top 5 of each 100-player group promote weekly |
| Legend II | Weekly Tournament | 30 battles/week | Top 3 of each group promote weekly |
| Legend I | 4-week Tournament | 8 battles/day | Players ranked below a cutoff demote weekly |

Battle Modifiers scale up through the tiers — Legend I applies the largest defensive-side bonus and the largest attacking-Hero penalty, making top-tier defense noticeably tougher to crack than Legend III.

The April 2026 update also relaxed inactivity rules significantly: players now get roughly **4 weeks** before any demotion begins (previously much shorter), with demotion afterward limited to one rank per additional 4 weeks rather than an immediate drop.

> **Advanced** — [VERIFY] Exact per-tier attack caps and promotion cutoffs are drawn from Supercell's own April 2026 blog post but third-party summaries show minor inconsistencies; confirm current numbers in-game before publishing them as unchangeable facts on the site.

*(Sources: Supercell official blog — Big Changes Coming to Ranked (April 2026), Battle and Ranked Modes Are Coming To Clash; Clash of Clans Fandom Wiki — Ranked Battles, Legend League Tournaments pages; GamingOnPhone League Floors explainer.)*

## 13. Economy Systems & Recurring Events

### Gold Pass (Season Challenges)

The paid seasonal pass, reworked in March 2026: instead of an expiring challenge list, **8 always-available Daily Tasks** earn Stamps toward a stackable Stamp Card, feeding a **40-tier reward track** where some tiers let players choose between two rewards. The old Season Bank was replaced by the **Hoggy Bank**, which starts at full size immediately and scales with Town Hall level; owning the Gold Pass applies a 5x payout multiplier when it's cashed out at season's end.

> **F2P Note** — Non-paying players still fill and claim the Hoggy Bank and complete Daily Tasks for track progress; they simply don't get the 5x cash-out multiplier or the tier's paid-only rewards.

### The Trader & Magic Items

The **Trader** appears once a village reaches Town Hall 6, offering Magic Items for Gems, event currency, or Raid Medals, with offers rotating weekly. Magic Items include:

| Category | Examples | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Books | Book of Fighting, Building, Spells, Heroes, Everything | Instantly complete one in-progress upgrade. |
| Potions | Power, Resource, Training, Builder, Hero Potions | Temporary boosts to troop stats, resource production, builder speed, or hero cooldowns. |
| Runes | Rune of Gold, Elixir, Dark Elixir, Builder Gold, Builder Elixir | Instantly fill the matching storage. |
| Hammers | Hammer of Fighting, Building, Spells, Heroes | Instantly max a troop/building/spell/hero to the highest level currently unlockable — stronger than a Book, which only completes what's already in progress. |
| Misc | Wall Ring, Shovel of Obstacles | Instantly upgrade one Wall segment, or remove an obstacle without needing a free Builder. |

### Star Bonus

A daily resource bonus (Gold, Elixir, Dark Elixir, and at higher Town Halls, Ore) awarded once 5 Stars are earned from attacking other villages within a rolling 24-hour window, scaling with Town Hall level and league.

### Friendly Challenges

Any clan member can post a no-risk challenge in clan chat — no Trophy loss, no resource loss, no Shield consumption — making it the standard way to test a new base layout or a new attack composition before using it for real.

### Resource & Gem Management

Farming (protecting/generating loot) and pushing (protecting Trophies/league rank) are somewhat opposed goals — a farming base often exposes the Town Hall to bait an easy partial win while burying storages, while a pushing base protects the core at the expense of storages. A common approach is to farm steadily at a comfortable Trophy range to fund upgrades, then push once resource needs for the current upgrade queue are met.

> **Priority** — For new or gem-conservative players, the highest-value Gem purchase in the game is unlocking additional Builders — going from 4 to 5 (and eventually a 6th via Builder Base's B.O.B. progression) permanently speeds up the entire upgrade queue. Community guidance consistently ranks this above speeding up individual upgrades or buying resources directly.

**Shields** are earned automatically when a base is significantly destroyed in a casual Battles-mode attack, temporarily preventing further attacks; a "Guard" state extends this protection while still letting the player attack freely. A Personal Break Timer prevents indefinite invulnerability from simply staying logged in.
